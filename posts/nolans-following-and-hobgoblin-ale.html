Nolan's Following: the Beginning of a Master

Hobgoblin English Ale

Following is a 1998 neo-noir drama set in London that traces the lives of two mysterious strangers: a young writer and a thief. If you like the kind of movies that hinge on dialogue and character dynamics as the driving force, then definitely check this one out. The movie delves into the mind of a timid, aspiring writer named Bill who begins following interesting strangers in order to quell his boredom and spark some literary inspiration. What starts out as an innocent habit, veers off into the unexpected as Bill decides to follow a specific man more than once.

Quite suddenly, the man ends up confronting Bill in a coffee shop fully aware of what he’s been doing. Thing is, this intelligent, well-dressed and self-confident man named Cobb happens to live an even more peculiar life as he’s a burglar who share’s Bill’s interest of following. These are the inciting events that get the ball rolling on this case study on how one stranger can get entangled in the spiral of private and public lives of others to his own detriment.

The perfect beer for this movie is Hobgoblin English Ale. It’s bold, amber in colour and has a mix of smooth and rich flavours that give it that element of complexity. Just like with the movie, with a bit of reflection on digging deeper below the first impression, there are underlying tones that bring a new appreciation of the whole when picked up.

Not only is it reason enough to see this movie for its gripping and nuanced plot, but it also happens to be Christopher Nolan’s first feature film (the same director who created Inception and The Dark Knight).
